02 · Architecture Pillars

Five Protocols. One Operating Contract.

Each pillar is a production requirement encoded in the core runtime—from North Sea platforms to lunar ISRU lines.

MQTT

MQTT Telemetry Core

ISO pub/sub with 2-byte headers and QoS 0–2. Engineered for bursty RF links, plant EMI, and satellite handoffs where TCP backoff wastes contact time.

  • BVLOS drone corridors
  • Remote pipeline and pump arrays
  • LEO ground-station relay chains
OPC UA

OPC UA Semantic Bridge

Certified client/server maps Siemens, ABB, and Schneider tags into our state graph—variables, alarms, and methods without per-site custom drivers.

  • Brownfield plant integration
  • OEM-agnostic operator HMIs
  • GxP and ISO audit trails
DTN

Delay-Tolerant Networking

NASA Bundle Protocol with store-and-forward buffers. Holds custody and telemetry through 4–22 minute signal delay and multi-day blackout windows.

  • Mars surface relay
  • Deep-space asset handoffs
  • Polar and maritime dead zones
CRDT

CRDT State Engine

Commutative merge lets rovers, vessels, and warehouses commit locally and reconcile later—no elected master, no split-brain after reconnect.

  • Autonomous haulage fleets
  • Multi-site inventory truth
  • Partition-tolerant ledgers
Edge AI

TinyModel Edge Partition

Splits vision and language workloads across ESP32, ARM gateways, and edge GPUs; bills inference by watt-hour at the site, not GPU-hour in a distant region.

  • Sub-100 ms anomaly detection
  • Federated model refresh
  • Neuromorphic reference firmware

How Authority Works

Vsevolod Ignatyev (@anriltine) holds genesis provenance—the root hash every release chain traces to. Day-to-day decisions merge through CRDT domains: runtime, product, protocol. Veto applies only to security releases, not to every stand-up.

How People Map to Nodes

Core engineers are peer replicas with full write rights in their shard. Partners enter as bridge nodes with scoped ACLs—not as owners of Links Space. Visitors and stargazers are QoS-0 subscribers: they receive telemetry, they do not mutate state.

What Must Never Recentralize

AI Transmitter observes; it never routes power. The hero graph must not redraw a king-sized node. Revenue flows through HSP on Links Space, not upward to charisma. Personal R&D upstreams via PR—experiments do not die in private forks.

How Nodes Interact Intellectually

Links are not only bytes and merge ops—each node publishes typed intent: protocol specs, custody semantics, inference partitions, and OPC UA models. Peers reconcile meaning through CRDT domains before state settles; bridge nodes translate vendor dialects without collapsing them into a hub language. AI Transmitter reads this intellectual layer read-only; it never authors truth.

Operating Mechanics

  1. Merge, not meeting. Conflicts resolve by last-write-wins within domain shards—runtime, ledger product, field protocol—not by who speaks loudest in a room.
  2. Upstream or expire. Experiments in personal repos merge into HyperlinksSpace org within one phase, or they are archived. No permanent shadow hubs.
  3. Offline is first-class. The private persistence layer (partner, home, rest) stores bundles when the founder disconnects. Links Space continues on last merged state.
  4. Observe, don’t orchestrate. Web, Strategy site, and Telegram surfaces subscribe to truth—they are not in the safety path, matching the architecture section above.
  5. Meaning merges before muscle. Runtime shards exchange signed protocol contracts and semantic bridges—not slideshows or DMs. When two nodes disagree on intent, the conflict resolves in the protocol domain, not in a private chat.
